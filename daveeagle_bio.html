About Dave
Dave has been interested in astronomy and looked up with wonder at the sky ever since he can remember. As a very young boy he used to gaze up at the night sky and wonder how it all fitted together. That puzzle didn’t start to unravel properly until the age of 9 when he suddenly “discovered” The Plough for himself. Once he had a start on the sky he gradually learnt his way around the visible constellations and learning the positions of all the “Celestial Lollypops” like the Messier objects. Living in North London he first met other kindred souls at South West Herts Astronomy Society. One look at Saturn through their large reflector had him hooked even more. As a teenager his attention was directed elsewhere, but his interest in astronomy was rekindled in 1985 when, as a newly married man and with his daughter well on the way, Halley’s comet returned and he bought his first serious telescope, a 3 inch refractor.

In 1987 he established the Bedford Astronomical Society where I built some wonderful friendships.  He was Editor of the Handbook of the Federation of Astronomical Societies for a few years and was elected a Fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society in 1996. He has been the lead astronomer for Hale-Bopp and Aurora flights for members of the public.

He has appeared regularly on local radio discussing many different aspects of astronomy and he has written articles for many of the British astronomy publications and supplied images to illustrate astronomy articles for photographic magazines, most recently two articles on astrophotography for Sky At Night Magazine. In 2013 he had his first astronomy book published by Springer and he has since written and published his own guides which accompany his successful and popular workshops. Dave also gives talks and presentations to schools, social groups, youth groups and astronomical societies or anyone else who is interested in finding out more about the night sky. He is also a STEMNET Ambassador.
