About Pete
Pete embarked on his astronomical journey in 1967 when he started Hagley RC Secondary School and noticed an observatory in a garden near the school playing fields in the village of Churchill Worcestershire. The observatory was owned by Mr Brian Manning a noted discoverer of asteroids, who then took Pete under his wing.

He joined the Kidderminster Astronomical Society where he had to a travel 46 miles round trip by bus to the meetings. He got his first telescope when he was 11 years old and at aged 12 he went on to build his own 6” reflector.  After leaving school 1973 he continued with astronomy and became quite proficient in the art of Astro Photography using hypered film and dark room techniques.

A move to the dark skies of rural Shropshire in 1989 meant that astronomy was high on the agenda once again. He was the founder of The Shropshire Astronomical Society and was elected at Fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society in 1994. In 2012 Pete began studying astronomy at UCLAN and Liverpool John Moors University.

Pete presents BBC Radio Shropshire’s Eye on the Sky radio show every month. He does solar imaging and also deep sky imaging with robotic telescope networks and has had image published in major astronomical journals and books around the world. He is the Deep Sky Imaging consultant with the Faulkes Telescope Educational Project. He also founded Astro Radio and organises the annual Solarsphere Astronomical and Music Festival.
